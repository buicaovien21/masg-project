Organisational Background (What is the key business/activity of the host organisation): 
 
Mount Alexander Sustainability Group (MASG) is the peak sustainability organisation for the Mount 
Alexander Shire, delivering education, research, advocacy, and endorsement for shire wide 
sustainability and clean energy initiatives. MASG was established in 2006 by a passionate group of 
locals who wanted coordinated action on climate change and to support the Mount Alexander Shire 
Community to work towards a sustainable future.  
Our 4 key focus areas are: 
- Waste Reduction 
- Renewable Energy 
- Energy Efficiency 
- Agriculture

Project Title: Developing the Strength, Viability and Connectedness of MASG.  
 
Problem statement/background: 
This section provides a detailed 
description of the issue or challenge 
that the project aims to address, 
including relevant context and 
background information. 
1. How to make volunteering in sustainability / 
regeneration community projects accessible, enticing 
and viewed as a worthy investment of time/energy to 
people aged between 16 and 30? 
 
2. Financial sustainability is an ongoing limitation of MASG, 
constantly dependent on securing external funding and 
membership fees. We’d like to explore social enterprise 
options for MASG with potential to develop an income 
stream to support our activities, whilst also engaging 
with community, in alignment with our values, mission 
and focus areas. 
 
3. In our modern world, communication is diverse in 
platforms, mediums, messaging and audience. We seek 
to better understand how to reach multi-generational 
audiences, towards increasing diverse engagement with 
our organisation, projects, events and happenings. What 
could be the best strategies to achieve this? 
 
Project Scope:

Key Research Questions 
• What factors influence youth perceptions of 
volunteering in sustainability-related initiatives? 
• Which social enterprise opportunities best align with 
MASG’s strengths, community context, and 
environmental goals? 
• How can digital, social, and creative communication 
strategies foster cross-generational engagement? 
 
Project Scope: 
1. Environmental impact, sustainability, and ecological 
regeneration 
• Conduct research on best practices and sustainability 
initiatives implemented by comparable organisations.

• Undertake benchmarking analysis to identify 
performance gaps and opportunities for improvement. 
• Perform a literature review of evidence-based 
community based sustainability environmental 
regeneration strategies and compile findings into a 
research report with actionable recommendations. 
2. Market Analysis 
• Investigate viable social enterprise models aligned with 
MASG’s mission and values. 
• Analyse and compare pricing and revenue structures 
across the sector. 
• Evaluate and report on financial sustainability 
strategies, focusing on long-term viability and 
scalability. 
3. Technology and Innovation 
• Research and benchmark the technological tools and 
platforms used by peer and partner organisations.  
• Evaluate technology pricing models and assess cost-
effectiveness. 
• Design an app layout (no prototype) that supports 
community engagement and education/engagement 
with sustainability and regenerative practice. 
• Assess the impact and effectiveness of existing and 
emerging technologies within the sector. 
4. Marketing and Community Engagement 
• Develop marketing campaigns that communicate 
MASG’s vision and achievements effectively. 
• Create community narratives and storytelling 
frameworks to inspire emotional connection and 
participation. 
• Strengthen branding and visual identity to enhance 
public recognition and trust. 
 
 
**All of these need to maintain a connection with MASG being 
a NFP, community guided-driven-led grassroots organisation, 
with a focus on us not just doing for community, but with 
community.
